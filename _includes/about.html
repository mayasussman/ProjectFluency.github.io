About Project Fluency

The Problem

There are 23 million adults in the United States with limited English proficiency, 90% of them immigrants. For them, language limits day-to-day life, access to jobs they are otherwise skilled for, even the ability to help their kids succeed in school.

The current solution, ESL (English as a Second Language) courses, only enroll 4% of the population in need, and are notorious for poor quality--only 40% of ESL students make progress year-to-year. Technology-based language learning programs are designed for a very different audience--much more suitable to short-term travel rather than long-term life.

Our Mission

We need to do better.

Project Fluency helps immigrants working in service jobs turn everyday workplace interactions into learning opportunities. In order to do this, we need to create a feeling of safety so people with limited proficiency can begin having conversations in English.

The Solution

We help small groups self-administer a brief curriculum focused on informal conversation, and augment it with SMS-based behavioral nudges, leading to real-world interactions in English. The result is a positive feedback loop that improves both confidence and proficiency over time.


Content built to encourage real-life conversations


Daily nudges to encourage practice in the real world


Virtual lesson delivery with minimal logistic overhead



The Value Proposition


Wins are the in the real world!


Small wins build self-efficacy (confidence).


Creates comfort by making learning social.
